BMI is a simple and convenient rule of thumb that is used to broadly categorize a person as being underweight, normal weight, over weight or obese. A regular use of the BMI is to assess how far someones body weight differs from what is normal for an individuals height. Below is a table showing the different levels of BMI.
Body fat percentage is a measure of fitness level, since it is the only body measurement which directly calculates a persons relative body composition without regard to height or weight. This calculation is based on gender, BMI and age.
Ideal Weight is based on height, gender, and age. There are several popular formulas for calculating ideal weight. It is intended to be used as a general guideline along with other useful personal health information.
Knowing your BMR helps you determine the estimated baseline amount of calories your body needs to function and serves as a starting point to determine how many calories you may want to consume based on your goals.
